response to Space Food Experiences: Designing Passenger's Eating Experiences for Future Space Travel Scenarios

Frontiers | Space Food Experiences: Designing Passenger's Eating Experiences for Future Space Travel Scenarios | Computer Science

"It is known, however, that food has a multi-dimensional and multi-sensorial role in societies and that sensory, hedonic, and social features of eating and food design should not be underestimated." - This reads as an herculean effort to describe culture using only its physiological efforts. The task of creating hedonic food in non-earth environments is identical to the task meeting the logistical demands of reproducing food culture non-earth environments.


"(given future space tourism)" - The work is based on the combination of assumed inevitability of the luxury space-tourism industry and the utility the future existence of this industry grants to this research effort. I have no personal opinion about the timeliness of this new luxury industry, but one wonders about the effects that poor food quality would have on a hypothetical luxury space tourism market. Would it drive away potential revenue? Would it save the industry operating costs? Would the industry be unable to maintain financial viability and disappear? What would the societal effects of a failed luxury space-tourism industry be?


The article repeatedly mentions a few qualities that an HCI-inspired approach to food products is uniquely able to generate such as:

Reflection - Reactive to daily physical activity data. However it is unclear whether presenting data according to subjectively created mappings is more illustrative of the data than other methods, such as providing documentation about the nuances and shortcoming of the data collection method.

Surprise - such 2d to 3d pasta shapes. But how many times can you be surprised by the same foodstuff? How many times would one want to be surprised with the same foodstuff? The majority of popular pasta shapes have reached ubiquity because of their physical reaction to being cooked, as well as cheap method of creation (usually by hand or high-scale mechanization) so it is unclear to me how an last-mile extrusion process has advantages in the world of pasta-shape making


Experiment Design design work was crowdsourced and word cloud generated. Words associated to categories to aid in numeric (summing) operation with assumption towards mutually-exclusivity.


Spice Bomb Mixing - to address the problem of liquid seasonings not being "fun" yet being practical they used solid shapes placed into a container for shaking. The solids somehow dissolve in a way that do not exhibit the same messiness of liquids or particle seasoning? But I am confused as to how.


3d Food Printer - Do xyz assemblies word in microgravity? How many source material reservers are needed? In the picture it only showed three. The "Functional, Sensorial, Emotional, Social" seem like objectives, not descriptions of how these proposals achieve any of those dimensions.


Earth Memory Bites - Selling 360 video and printing standardized packets of combinatory flavored balls is proposed as a solution to homesickness.
